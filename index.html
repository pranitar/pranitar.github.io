FeedMe

FeedMe references ‘food.json’ on the site server to access a list of popular and suggested Instagram tags. The list of recommended tags is accessed when the user clicks on “Recommended”, instantiating the ajax call to ‘food.json’ and populating the list. In the text input, users are able to enter a single tag describing a food or craving. After pressing submit or pressing enter, FeedMe retrieves a list of the 20 most-recent Instagram images containing the entered tag via JSONP. The right side of the screen is then populated with the images. Users can browse their results and click on images to be redirected to the Instagram user who posted the image.

Where To Eat

We have created, Where to Eat, a scalable web application that will help users find places to eat on Carnegie Mellon's campus and the neighboring Craig Street based on a variety of parameters including location, cost, approximate wait time, and reviews from other users. The typical ‘Where to Eat’ user would be a Carnegie Mellon professor, visitor, or student (graduate and undergraduate).The quality of restaurants (food-wise, service-wise, price-wise, etc.) is maintained through user ratings and reviews of these locations.



CMU Tanah

This is the website I designed for Tanah, CMU's all-girls South Asian fusion dance team. One of the core values of the team is female empowerment and therefore, the team's official colors are black and pink. I played with the contrast in light from our performance pictures to frame different text and image sections of the website.
